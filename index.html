Is your dog in trouble? Act fast, the right way.

DogEmergency.org helps you recognise urgent warning signs, prepare for the clinic call, and understand vet-guided recovery support — for bloat, poisoning, heatstroke, the gut, and getting your dog back to health.

Go to a vet now if your dog has any of these

  • A swollen, hard belly and is retching without bringing anything up (possible bloat / GDV)
  • Collapsed, very weak, or has pale, white or blue gums
  • Trouble breathing, choking, or gasping
  • A seizure lasting more than a few minutes, or repeated seizures
  • Eaten something toxic (chocolate, xylitol, grapes/raisins, rodenticide, medicines)
  • Heavy bleeding, a road accident, or a major fall

When in doubt, call an emergency animal hospital and describe the signs — they will tell you whether to come straight in.

Why act first, not later

Some dog emergencies — bloat, poisoning, heatstroke, breathing trouble — can become life-threatening within minutes to hours. The safest move is almost always to call a veterinarian early and let them decide, rather than waiting to see whether it settles. This site helps you recognise the red flags and act calmly; it does not replace a veterinarian.
